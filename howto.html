Hello! I'm here to help!

     Here's whats going on...

 

You should select your desired mode via the drop-down selection menu. Modes are calculated on the average word length as well as how many special characters like capitals, numbers, and symbols are included in the text. As default, it will choose a test at random. Furthermore, if you just want random, chose 'Random' in the drop down! (or stay on Select, if you really want)

  

Your prompt will be given to you in the white box and your cursor will begin at the first letter of the first word. Words from the prompt may be cut short and moved to the next line. You may notice there are no hyphens when there are these word breaks; this is to avoid confusion during your test.

  

Begin typing whenever you’re ready and the 60-second timer will begin to count down. Type for as fast as you can, for as long as you can, until the timer runs out.

  

You can fix errors as you go, correct them at the end, or not at all - it’s up to you! Fixing errors can improve your accuracy, but can lower your WPM/CPM.

  

If you need to restart the test, press the ‘Try Again’ button, under the prompt area, at any time. Interactive feedback shows you your current wpm and accuracy.

  

At the end of the test, your results will remain under the prompt until you refresh the page or press ‘Try Again’.

  

Selecting the Right Test Mode for You

      Mode Selection Help

  

Easy Mode:

 Short, simple sentences with straightforward vocabulary. Ideal for beginners or those who prefer a relaxed typing experience.

Who Should Choose This Mode:

   ~Beginners or those new to typing tests.

   ~Individuals who want a low-stress practice session.

   ~People looking to warm up before attempting more challenging tests.

  

Medium Mode:

 Slightly more complex sentences with moderate vocabulary. Suitable for those with some typing experience who want a moderate challenge.

Who Should Choose This Mode:

   ~Intermediate typists looking to improve their skills.

   ~Those who find the Easy Mode too simple but are not ready for advanced paragraphs.

   ~Individuals aiming to enhance their typing speed and accuracy with a balanced difficulty.

  

Hard Mode:

 Complex sentences with advanced vocabulary and diverse sentence structures. Designed for experienced typists seeking a significant challenge.

Who Should Choose This Mode:

   ~Advanced typists who are confident in their typing abilities.

   ~Individuals preparing for professional typing requirements or competitive typing tests.

   ~Typists who enjoy challenging themselves with difficult content.

  

Very Hard Mode:

 Highly complex sentences with sophisticated vocabulary and intricate sentence structures. Suitable for expert typists who want the ultimate test of their skills.

Who Should Choose This Mode:

   ~Expert typists looking to push their limits.

   ~Those preparing for high-stakes typing competitions or specialized professional roles.

   ~Individuals who excel in Hard Mode and seek an even greater challenge.
